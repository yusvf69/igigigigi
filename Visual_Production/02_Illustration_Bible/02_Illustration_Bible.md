## 1. Story Scenes (Historical Events)

### Purpose
Depict pivotal moments in Islamic history with cinematic composition and period-accurate detail. These illustrations serve as visual anchors for educational content, bringing historical narratives to life for learners. Each scene must communicate the emotional weight, cultural context, and educational significance of the event while maintaining visual engagement.

### Style
Cinematic illustration with painterly rendering. Combines digital painting techniques with reference to Islamic miniature painting traditions (Persian, Mughal, Ottoman) for stylistic authenticity. Uses atmospheric perspective to create depth. Figures are stylized realistically with expressive poses. Backgrounds are richly detailed but composed to prioritize narrative clarity over decorative excess.

### Perspective
Dynamic perspective chosen per scene to maximize emotional impact and informational clarity. Bird's-eye for battle formations and crowd scenes. Eye-level for intimate character moments. Three-quarter aerial for treaty signings and scholarly debates. Avoid extreme foreshortening that obscures educational content. Maintain consistent vanishing points within a single scene.

### Composition Rules
- Rule of thirds for primary subject placement
- Leading lines (architecture, landscape features, weapon lines, pointing gestures) toward focal point
- Foreground/midground/background depth layering
- Crowd scenes: establish hierarchy through size, position, and color saturation of key figures
- Intimate scenes: tighter framing, shallow depth of field, focus on faces and hands
- Negative space used to direct attention and convey scale
- Diagonal composition for battle and conflict scenes
- Horizontal/vertical stability for treaty and scholarly scenes
- Golden ratio positioning for primary emotional beats
- Frame-within-frame using architectural elements (arches, doorways, windows)

### Color Palette
- **Battle scenes:** Warm earth tones (burnt sienna, ochre, umber) with strategic cool accent colors (steel blue, teal) for armor and sky. Red for blood and banners. Gold for leadership figures.
- **Treaty signings:** Rich jewel tones (lapis lazuli, emerald green, deep crimson) with gold and ivory accents. Balanced warm and cool harmonies.
- **Scholarly debates:** Warm amber and parchment tones with deep blue and green accents. Softer contrast for intellectual atmosphere.
- **Daily life:** Varied by region and period. Earthy pigment palette referencing historical color availability. Muted saturation for authenticity with strategic saturated accents.
- **Night scenes:** Deep indigo and teal with warm gold and amber light sources (torches, lamps, moonlight). High contrast between light and shadow.

### Lighting Model
Single dominant light source with secondary fill. Sunlight for outdoor scenes with warm directional shadows. Torch/lamp light for indoor and night scenes with falloff and color temperature shift from warm center to cool edge. Atmospheric scattering for dust, smoke, and humidity. Backlighting for silhouette emphasis in dramatic moments. Soft diffused light for scholarly and domestic interiors.

### Mood
Determined by event emotional profile. Somber and reverent for tragedies. Triumphant and energetic for victories. Contemplative and dignified for scholarly exchanges. Warm and nostalgic for daily life. Tension and urgency for impending conflict. Serenity for moments of peace and resolution.

### Camera Angle
- **Low angle:** Heroic figures, monumental scale, power and authority
- **High angle:** Vulnerability, overview of crowd/formation, tactical clarity
- **Eye level:** Intimate connection, character identification, emotional access
- **Dutch angle:** Instability, chaos, battle disorientation
- **Over-the-shoulder:** Subjective viewpoint, participant perspective
- **Wide establishing:** Context and setting orientation

### Visual Hierarchy
1. Primary subject (key figure or action): highest contrast, sharpest detail, most saturated
2. Secondary figures: moderate detail, supporting color palette
3. Environment context: atmospheric, simplified detail, color-harmonized
4. Background elements: muted, low contrast, atmospheric perspective

### Animation Possibilities
- Slow camera pan across wide scenes
- Parallax depth separation for immersive feel
- Particle effects: dust, smoke, falling leaves, snow
- Animated light sources: flickering torches, changing sunlight
- Subtle figure animation: breathing, gentle movement, cloth physics
- Scene transitions: dissolve, zoom-in to detail, wipe across geometric pattern
- Battle scenes: arrow trajectories, flag movement, dust clouds
- Water animation: rivers, fountains, rain

### Accessibility Considerations
- Minimum contrast ratio 4.5:1 for all informational elements
- Avoid conveying information through color alone — use shape, position, and labels
- Sufficient detail separation between figure and background
- Alternative text descriptions for all scenes (50-150 words describing action, setting, and educational significance)
- Motion sensitivity: provide reduced-motion alternative for animated versions
- Consider red-green color blindness in palette choices (avoid red-on-green critical information)
- Text overlays use background shielding for readability
- Scalable vector annotations for figures and objects of interest

### Educational Goal
Enable learners to visualize historical events with spatial, temporal, and cultural accuracy. Support narrative memory formation through emotionally resonant imagery. Provide visual reference for event chronology, participant identification, and geographical context. Facilitate teacher explanations and student recall through memorable compositional framing.

### Do Examples
- Battle of Badr: aerial perspective showing Muslim and Quraysh formations, angelic host faintly indicated in sky, date palm grove framing, strategic well positions visible, expressive faces of key figures (Hamza, Ali, Abu Jahl), dust and energy in composition
- Treaty of Hudaybiyyah: balanced bilateral composition, Prophet center-right with companions, Suhayl ibn Amr center-left with Quraysh delegation, neutral ground between, simple setting to emphasize diplomatic moment, calm lighting, dignified postures
- House of Wisdom debate: circular/radial composition with scholars arranged in discussion circle, books and instruments visible, Caliph al-Mamun positioned as patron, warm lamp lighting, layered architecture showing library depth, animated gesture of debate
- Ibn Sina treating patients: intimate examination scene, patient on divan, Ibn Sina with diagnostic instruments, assistant preparing remedies, books of canon visible, soft window light, compassionate expressions

### Don't Examples
- Anachronistic objects, clothing, or architecture
- Caricature or disrespectful depiction of prophets or companions (follow Islamic guidelines)
- Modern perspective or artistic conventions that break historical immersion
- Overly dark or indistinct figures that confuse narrative
- Gratuitous violence or gore in battle scenes
- Western fantasy art stylistic influences (no horned helmets, plate armor in wrong period)
- Cluttered composition with no clear focal point
- Facial depictions of prophets (follow agreed institutional guidelines on depiction)

### Production Specifications
- **Canvas size:** 3840 x 2160 px (4K UHD) for hero scenes; 1920 x 1080 px for inline content
- **DPI:** 300 DPI for print; 72 DPI for digital
- **File format:** PSD (working), PNG (deliverable), WebP (web), JPEG (thumbnail)
- **Layers:** Organized layer groups: Background, Environment, Architecture, Figures, Foreground, Lighting FX, Color Grading
- **Color space:** sRGB for digital; CMYK for print
- **Max file size:** 50 MB PSD; 5 MB PNG; 500 KB WebP
- **Source files:** Include all adjustment layers, masks, and smart objects
- **Naming convention:** SS_Category_EventName_Version_Date (e.g., SS_Battle_Badr_v01_20260601)

### Review Criteria
- Historical accuracy verified against primary sources
- Composition guides eye to primary subject within 2 seconds
- Color palette appropriate to period and mood
- All figures properly proportioned and positioned
- Lighting model consistent throughout scene
- Educational goal clearly served by visual choices
- No anachronisms present
- Islamic depiction guidelines followed
- Accessibility contrast requirements met
- Animation notes clear and actionable
- File organization meets production standards
- Approved by historical consultant

## 2. Historical Environments

### Purpose
Create immersive, period-accurate historical environments that establish context for educational content. These wide establishing shots transport learners to specific times and places, providing spatial understanding of historical settings. Environments must communicate the scale, atmosphere, architectural character, and daily life of each location while supporting the educational narrative.

### Style
Panoramic environmental illustration combining architectural precision with atmospheric painting. Inspired by historical city views (Matta, Piri Reis, Ottoman miniatures) with modern digital painting techniques. Balance between documentary accuracy and artistic evocation. Detail density varies by importance — focal areas receive higher detail than peripheral zones.

### Perspective
Wide establishing shots predominately use high-angle or bird's-eye perspective for maximum informational density. Three-point perspective for dramatic vertical elements (minarets, towers). One-point for axial compositions (grand courtyards, processional ways). Atmospheric perspective creates depth through color and value shifts. Maintain consistent horizon line across related environment sets.

### Composition Rules
- Foreground framing elements (architectural details, trees, hills) to create depth
- Clear horizontal banding: sky, middle distance, foreground
- Leading lines: roads, waterways, city walls, colonnades
- Rule of thirds for key architectural landmarks
- S-curve composition for markets and processional routes
- Scale indicators: human figures, animals, everyday objects
- Negative space for sky and open areas
- Balance between built environment and natural setting
- Golden ratio for positioning major architectural focal points
- Layered depth: at least 4 distinct depth planes

### Color Palette
- **Baghdad (Round City):** Pale ochre walls, golden desert light, blue Tigris, green gardens, white marble palaces, earth tones with turquoise dome accents
- **Cordoba:** Warm Andalusian gold, whitewashed buildings, blue sky, green vegetation, orange tile roofs, silver olive groves
- **Damascus:** Grey-green Ghouta gardens, golden stone, blue mosaic accents, red roof tiles, brown earth
- **Cairo:** Warm limestone, dusty rose, Nile blue, green palm fronds, gold sand, white Mamluk stone
- **Jerusalem:** Golden Jerusalem stone, deep blue sky, grey olive trees, white Dome of the Rock, terra cotta roofs
- **Fez:** Deep earth tones, brown and beige walls, green rooftops, blue sky, terra cotta, white minarets
- **Samarkand:** Turquoise domes, blue tile work, golden desert, white marble, green gardens, deep night sky
- **Istanbul:** Blue Bosphorus, red roofs, grey stone, green hills, gold mosaics, white minarets

### Lighting Model
Natural sunlight primary source. Time of day selected for optimal architectural presentation — typically golden hour (sunrise/sunset) for warm, dramatic shadows, or morning light for clear detail visibility. Shadow length and direction consistent with sun position. Diffuse skylight for overcast atmosphere options. Artificial light sources for night scenes (lanterns, torches, illuminated windows). Light scattering for atmospheric haze, dust, and humidity.

### Mood
Determined by the city's role in the educational context. Bustling and vibrant for commercial centers. Serene and sacred for religious cities. Intellectual and contemplative for centers of learning. Powerful and imposing for imperial capitals. Mysterious and exotic to engage curiosity. Nostalgic and reverent for historical reflection.

### Camera Angle
- **Bird's-eye (1000-2000m):** Complete city layout, urban planning, relationship between districts
- **High angle (100-300m):** City panorama with recognizable landmarks, human activity visible
- **Medium establishing (50-100m):** Street scene scale, building facades, market activity
- **Street level:** Ground perspective, daily life immersion, human-scale experience
- **From water:** City approach by river or sea, walls and waterfront
- **From high point (minaret/hill):** Viewpoint a historical resident might have experienced

### Visual Hierarchy
1. Dominant landmark(s): highest contrast, most detail, architectural focal point
2. City/district character: secondary contrast, defining architectural style
3. Human activity: small scale but vital for life and scale reference
4. Natural setting: terrain, water, vegetation providing context
5. Atmosphere and sky: lighting, weather, time of day indicators

### Animation Possibilities
- Slow panoramic camera move
- Day-to-night transition or time-lapse
- Cloud movement and changing sky
- Water animation (rivers, fountains, harbor activity)
- People and traffic movement
- Flags waving, sails moving
- Light changes — sun position, shadow movement
- Zoom from establishing shot into specific location
- Seasonal transitions
- Bird flocks, smoke from chimneys

### Accessibility Considerations
- High contrast between architectural elements and sky
- Clear silhouette readability for city skyline
- Alternative text: 100-200 words describing location, period, key architectural features, atmosphere
- Avoid conveying time period through color alone — use architectural style as primary indicator
- Motion sensitivity reduced-motion variant for animated establishing shots
- Label key landmarks with high-contrast text callouts
- Color-blind accessible palette for informational overlays

### Educational Goal
Provide immediate spatial and cultural context for historical content. Enable learners to understand city layout, architectural character, and relationship between human activity and built environment. Support mental mapping of historical geography. Create memorable visual anchors for location-based learning.

### Do Examples
- Baghdad Round City: concentric circular walls from bird's-eye, four gates aligned with cardinal directions, central mosque and palace, Tigris River curving past, suburbs and gardens outside walls, bridges connecting east and west, human scale at city gates
- Cordoba: Great Mosque visible at city center, Roman bridge crossing Guadalquivir, walled medina with winding streets, palace complex (Alcazar), hillside suburbs, olive groves beyond, spring flowering trees
- Cairo (Mamluk period): Citadel on hill dominating skyline, Muizz Street spine, dense urban fabric, mosques with twin minarets, Nile visible to west, Muqattam hills to east, cemetery city visible at edges
- Samarkand: Registan ensemble as focal point from high angle, turquoise domes of Gur-e-Amir and Bibi-Khanym, blue tile work catching sunlight, gardens within city walls, surrounding steppe, mountain backdrop

### Don't Examples
- Mixing architectural styles from different periods
- Modern elements (electrical wires, cars, modern clothing)
- Inconsistent shadow direction across scene
- Flat, depthless composition
- Overly pristine environments without signs of life and use
- Western medieval city layout imposed on Islamic city
- Missing scale reference (no people or familiar objects)
- Color palette that contradicts historical pigment availability or regional character
- Anachronistic building materials (concrete, steel frame)

### Production Specifications
- **Canvas size:** 5760 x 3240 px for hero environments; 3840 x 2160 px for standard; 1920 x 1080 px for inline
- **DPI:** 300 DPI print; 72 DPI digital
- **File format:** PSD (working layered), PNG (deliverable), EXR (HDR if lighting to be reused)
- **Layers:** Sky/Atmosphere, Background Terrain, City Background, City Midground, City Foreground, Details/Figures, Lighting, Color Grading, Labels
- **Color space:** sRGB digital; Adobe RGB print
- **Reference board:** Included in PSD as reference layer group
- **Naming convention:** ENV_City_Period_Version_Date
- **Palette file:** .ase color palette included with deliverable

### Review Criteria
- Architectural accuracy for specified period
- Consistent atmospheric perspective
- Clear visual hierarchy with dominant focal point
- Appropriate time of day and lighting
- Human scale incorporated for size reference
- Color palette matches regional and period character
- Educational context reinforced by environment choices
- No anachronisms
- Accessibility standards met
- Animatable elements identified and documented
- File structure meets production standards
- Reviewed by architecture and history consultants

## 3. Architecture

### Purpose
Document and illustrate individual buildings and structures from Islamic architectural heritage with educational precision. These illustrations serve as detailed references for architectural understanding, supporting lessons on design, engineering, cultural expression, and historical context. Cross-sections provide unique educational value by revealing internal spatial organization and structural systems.

### Style
Architectural illustration combining precision drawing with atmospheric rendering. Exterior views use painterly style with accurate proportional representation. Interior views employ warm, atmospheric lighting with attention to decorative programs. Cross-sections use clean line work with color-coded material indication and subtle atmospheric rendering. Detail close-ups emphasize craft and ornamentation with photographic reference quality.

### Perspective
- **Exteriors:** Two-point perspective for corner views; one-point for symmetrical facades; three-point for towers and monumental structures. True architectural perspective with careful vanishing point alignment.
- **Interiors:** One-point perspective for axial spaces (prayer halls, iwans); two-point for corner views of rooms and courtyards. Wide-angle (70-90mm equivalent) for spatial comprehension.
- **Cross-sections:** Orthographic projection (1:100 or 1:200 scale) with elevation cut-through. Clean vector lines with rendered fills.
- **Detail close-ups:** Macro perspective with shallow depth of field, 1:1 scale representation of decorative elements.

### Composition Rules
- **Exterior:** Building centered or offset by rule of thirds. Sky and ground providing context. Leading lines from pathways, roads, or landscape. Balance between building mass and negative space.
- **Interior:** Strong axial composition for symmetrical spaces. Frame-within-frame through arches, doorways, screens. Foreground architectural elements creating depth.
- **Cross-section:** Building centered in frame. Clear labeling system with leader lines. Consistent scale bar. Material legend. North arrow and orientation.
- **Detail:** Subject fills 70% of frame. Minimal background to avoid distraction. Scale reference included (coin, hand, ruler). Lighting from upper-left for relief clarity.

### Color Palette
- **Region-specific stone:** Arabian gold limestone, Egyptian ochre, Andalusian white, Syrian black basalt, Turkish grey stone, Indian red sandstone, Persian turquoise tile
- **Wood:** Warm browns — walnut, cedar, teak, oak — with grain indicated on detailed views
- **Tile:** Palette per region: blue/white (Iznik), turquoise/blue (Persian), green/yellow (Damascus), polychrome (Maghreb)
- **Interior:** Warm ambient light palette — amber, gold, cream, with saturated decorative accents
- **Cross-section:** Neutral grey base with color-coded materials: red/brick, blue/water, yellow/wood, green/garden
- **Decorative details:** Lapis lazuli, vermilion, gold leaf, emerald, turquoise — historically authentic pigment colors

### Lighting Model
- **Exteriors:** Sunlight at 45-degree angle for shadow detail on ornamentation. Golden hour for warm dramatic presentation. Cloudy diffused for uniform detail visibility.
- **Interiors:** Mixed natural light from windows/courtyard and artificial light from lamps. Light falloff creating depth. Warm color temperature for intimate atmosphere.
- **Cross-sections:** Flat, even lighting for technical clarity. No dramatic shadows that obscure information.
- **Detail close-ups:** Raking light for texture emphasis on carved surfaces. Diffused light for tile and mosaic. Specular highlight for metallic and glazed surfaces.

### Mood
Respectful and reverent for religious architecture. Intellectually stimulating for educational institutions. Awe-inspiring for monumental structures. Intimate and contemplative for domestic architecture. Curious and detailed for craft and ornamentation studies. Functional and clear for cross-sections.

### Camera Angle
- **Exterior:** Three-quarter view most common for volume understanding. Straight-on elevation for facade study. Worm's-eye for tower/minaret monumentality. Aerial for roofscape and dome clusters.
- **Interior:** Eye-level (1.5m) for human-scale experience. Low angle for dome interiors. High angle for floor pattern views and courtyard overviews from upper galleries.
- **Cross-section:** Orthographic, perpendicular to cut plane.
- **Detail:** Perpendicular to surface for flat ornament; 15-30 degree angle for carved relief.

### Visual Hierarchy
1. Primary structure: full building mass, highest contrast
2. Key architectural features: entrance, dome, minaret, mihrab — secondary contrast
3. Ornamentation and decorative programs: detail density, color saturation
4. Context: ground, sky, landscape, adjacent structures
5. Scale references: human figures, vegetation elements

### Animation Possibilities
- 360-degree rotation for exterior views
- Section cut fly-through: from exterior through interior spaces
- Construction sequence animation: building rising from foundation to completion
- Light study animation: sun positions throughout day
- Dome interior: rotating view of muqarnas and tile work
- Cross-section overlay: ghosted exterior fading to reveal interior
- Detail zoom: from establishing view to ornament close-up
- Decorative pattern assembly: geometric construction steps animated

### Accessibility Considerations
- Cross-section labels: minimum 12pt sans-serif font, high contrast leader lines
- Color coding includes pattern fills for grayscale/print accessibility
- Alt text: 75-150 words describing structure, period, location, key architectural features, and educational relevance
- Provide tactile-description-friendly text for complex spatial relationships
- Audio description for animated architecture tours
- Avoid conveying structural systems through color alone in cross-sections
- Font sizing for labels: minimum 14px digital, 10pt print

### Educational Goal
Develop architectural literacy in learners — ability to read plans, understand structural systems, recognize period and regional styles, and appreciate the relationship between form, function, and cultural expression. Support architecture and engineering lessons with accurate visual reference material.

### Do Examples
- Dome of the Rock: exterior establishing view with golden dome, octagonal structure, blue tile, city context; interior cross-section showing dome structure, ambulatory, central rock; detail of mosaic and tile bands; 3D cutaway showing foundation and water systems below
- Alhambra: Court of the Lions — one-point perspective from entrance arch, fountain as focal point, intricate columns, tile dados, upper rooms visible; detail of muqarnas dome in Hall of the Two Sisters with lighting simulation
- Hagia Sophia: Interior wide-angle from nave floor, dome seeming to float, light from windows, gallery level, marble panels; cross-section showing dome thrust system, buttresses, pendentives; detail of calligraphy roundels with scale
- Ibn Tulun Mosque: Exterior from courtyard showing spiral minaret, pointed arches, brick pilasters, fountain; cross-section of minaret showing internal staircase; detail of stucco carved decoration with raking light for depth
- Traditional courtyard house: Section showing rooms arranged around courtyard, fountain central, wind catcher, basement, roof terrace, layered privacy gradient from public to private

### Don't Examples
- Stylized or cartoon proportions that misrepresent architectural reality
- Missing structural context (arches without supporting elements)
- Inconsistent scale between architectural elements
- Western architectural vocabulary applied to Islamic buildings (Gothic arches, Roman columns where historically inaccurate)
- Modern interventions not noted (restoration, additions)
- Overexposed or underexposed renderings losing detail
- Cross-sections without scale bars or orientation
- Mixing decorative styles from different periods and regions
- Anachronistic landscaping or urban context

### Production Specifications
- **Canvas size:** Exteriors 3840 x 2160 px; Interiors 3840 x 2560 px; Cross-sections vector AI/EPS at A3 300 DPI; Details 2400 x 2400 px
- **DPI:** 300 DPI print; 72 DPI digital
- **File format:** PSD (painted), AI (cross-sections, vector ornament), PNG (deliverable), PDF (print-ready cross-sections)
- **Layers:** Exteriors — Sky, Background, Building Mass, Details, Ornament, Lighting, Atmosphere; Cross-sections — Cut Line, Structure, Materials, Labels, Scale
- **Reference:** Minimum 5 reference photos with annotations
- **Measurement annotations:** Included in separate layer for cross-sections
- **Naming convention:** ARCH_StructureName_ViewType_Version_Date
- **Scale:** Cross-sections always include scale bar and graphic scale

### Review Criteria
- Architectural accuracy verified against measured drawings and archaeological sources
- Proportional correctness of all architectural elements
- Cross-section accuracy: structural systems correctly represented
- Perspective construction: vanishing points correctly aligned
- Ornamentation accurately reproduced (pattern, color, placement)
- Scale consistent throughout
- Lighting appropriate to time and space
- Labels accurate and legible
- Educational value of the architectural choice clear
- Region and period styles correctly distinguished
- File meets production format standards
- Reviewed by architectural historian

## 4. Landscapes

### Purpose
Create evocative, atmospheric landscape illustrations that transport learners to the diverse natural environments of the Islamic world. These landscapes support geographical and historical education by showing the terrain in which Islamic civilization flourished. Each illustration communicates the character, mood, and ecological reality of a specific landscape type while inspiring wonder and connection to the natural world.

### Style
Painterly landscape illustration with atmospheric emphasis. Combines observational naturalism with the aesthetic traditions of Islamic landscape art — garden carpets, miniature painting terrain conventions, and Persian poetic landscape imagery. Color and light are primary communicators of mood and time. Detail density varies strategically with atmospheric perspective.

### Perspective
Broad panoramic views using wide-angle natural perspective (equivalent to 24-35mm lens). Three-plane depth system: foreground detail, midground form, background atmosphere. Horizon line placement determines sky-to-land ratio — lower horizons emphasize dramatic skies, higher horizons emphasize land forms. Aerial perspective with at least three distinct atmospheric layers.

### Composition Rules
- Strong foreground element for depth entry (rock, tree, dune, vegetation cluster)
- S-curve or diagonal leading lines through landscape (rivers, ridge lines, dune crests, paths)
- Golden ratio placement of focal point (remarkable tree, rock formation, water body)
- Balance of masses: sky mass vs land mass vs water mass
- Negative sky space for expansive, contemplative mood
- Scale indicators: birds, distant figures, vegetation
- Horizon line: never center (rule of thirds placement)
- Atmospheric depth: minimum 3 value planes (foreground dark, midground mid, background light)
- Color temperature shift: warm foreground to cool background (or reverse for sunset)
- Framing: natural elements (branches, grass, rock overhang) for depth

### Color Palette
- **Arabian desert:** Warm ochre, burnt sienna, gold, cream, terracotta, rose, deep indigo shadows, brilliant blue sky
- **Mountains (Hijaz/Alborz/Atlas):** Grey-blue rock, green vegetation, snow white, deep sky blue, purple distance, warm golden highlights
- **Valleys and rivers:** Emerald green vegetation, turquoise water, brown earth, blue sky, white clouds, golden sunlight
- **Oases:** Deep green date palms, blue water, warm sand, green reeds, gold date clusters
- **Coastlines:** Cyan water, white sand, blue sky, green coastal vegetation, warm rock, golden sunset reflection
- **Starry skies:** Deep indigo to black gradient, brilliant white/gold stars, blue-white Milky Way, warm horizon glow
- **Spring:** Fresh green, flowering colors (pink, white, yellow), bright blue sky, soft white clouds
- **Autumn:** Warm gold, amber, burnt orange, brown, muted green, soft grey-blue sky
- **Magic hour:** Gold, orange, pink, purple, deep blue, warm highlights, cool shadows

### Lighting Model
Natural sunlight as primary source with atmospheric effects. Clear sun for desert and mountain scenes. Diffuse overcast for valleys and forests. Golden hour (sunrise/sunset) for emotional landscape moments. Blue hour for twilight transitions. Night lighting from stars and moon for celestial landscapes. Light scattering and atmospheric haze for depth. Volumetric light rays through clouds, dust, or vegetation. Sun position determines shadow direction and length consistently.

### Mood
- **Desert:** Vast, timeless, contemplative, severe beauty, spiritual solitude
- **Mountains:** Majestic, awe-inspiring, challenging, sublime
- **Valleys:** Peaceful, fertile, nurturing, abundant
- **Oases:** Sanctuary, relief, life, water as gift
- **Coastlines:** Boundary, horizon, trade, connection, infinite
- **Starry skies:** Wonder, infinity, contemplation, celestial order
- **Spring:** Renewal, hope, beauty, life
- **Autumn:** Harvest, completion, warmth, transition
- **Storm:** Power, cleansing, drama, transformation

### Camera Angle
- **Panoramic (high angle):** Sweeping views, valley systems, coastline curves, dune seas
- **Eye level:** Immersive ground perspective, human connection to landscape
- **From elevation (mountain, hill):** Territory overview, journey context, scale of landscape
- **Low angle (looking up):** Monumental trees, cliffs, mountain peaks, sky emphasis
- **Aerial (drone equivalent):** Landscape patterns, water forms, terrain structure, vegetation systems

### Visual Hierarchy
1. Dominant landscape feature (mountain, water body, tree, dune): highest contrast and detail
2. Midground landforms and vegetation: secondary visual weight
3. Sky and atmosphere: mood and time of day establishing
4. Foreground detail: texture, scale, entry point for viewer
5. Life elements (birds, animals, figures): small but psychologically important

### Animation Possibilities
- Time-lapse: entire day cycle compressed
- Cloud movement across sky with changing shadows
- Water animation: waves, reflections, flowing rivers
- Wind effects: grass, trees, sand, fabric
- Star movement and moon phase progression
- Seasonal transition animation
- Sunrise to sunset color and shadow change
- Light rays moving across landscape
- Animal and bird movement
- Weather transitions (clear to storm, fog lifting)

### Accessibility Considerations
- High contrast between major landscape masses
- Clear value separation between foreground, midground, background
- Alt text: 75-150 words describing landscape type, time of day, season, notable features, atmosphere
- Color-independent depth cues: value, detail density, overlapping
- Motion sensitivity: reduced animation option for landscape transitions
- Audio description for animated landscapes noting visual and atmospheric details
- Consider protanopia/deuteranopia in landscape color choices — maintain contrast through value

### Educational Goal
Connect learners to the geographical reality of the Islamic world. Support understanding of how environment shaped civilization — trade routes, settlement patterns, agriculture, architecture, and cultural expression. Create emotional connection to place that enhances memory and engagement.

### Do Examples
- Empty Quarter at sunset: sweeping dune curves, long shadows, warm gold and orange sky, single camel and rider as scale, cool purple shadows on dune reverse slopes, atmospheric haze on horizon, infinite quality
- Hijaz mountain range: layered purple and blue peaks receding to distance, green wadi valley below, white clouds casting shadows, small fortress village clinging to slope, golden sunlight on foreground rock
- Andalusian olive grove: rolling green hills, silver-green olive trees in repeated pattern, white farmhouse in distance, spring wildflowers in foreground, soft overcast light, bird circling above
- Desert oasis: cluster of date palms, blue pool reflecting sky, reed edge, camel caravan approaching, warm sand all around, green contrast against gold, cool shade under palms, well with figures
- Night sky over the Atlas Mountains: deep indigo sky, brilliant stars clustered like dust, Milky Way band, mountain silhouette black against sky, single campfire warm glow in valley, moon crescent

### Don't Examples
- Generic landscape that could be anywhere — lacks regional character

### Perspective

- **Route maps:** Schematic line-based with waypoints, distance markers, and illustrated landmarks
- **Celestial maps:** Hemispheric projection with constellation figures illustrated in Islamic astronomical style
- **Ocean charts:** Coastline emphasis, port symbols, wind directions, sea monsters (historical style), rhumb lines
- **Battle maps:** Topographic base with troop movements, fortifications, key positions labeled

### Composition Rules
- Clear visual hierarchy from macro (continent/country) to micro (city, route segment)
- Decorative border frame with geometric or arabesque pattern
- Compass rose or qibla indicator in corner or margin
- Cartouche containing title, date, scale, legend
- Scale bar in multiple units (miles, farsakhs, days travel)
- Land and water differentiation through color, texture, and labeling
- Relief indication through consistent hachure, hill shading, or spot heights
- Place name labels with hierarchy (city size = font size)
- Route lines with consistent symbolic language
- Illustrated vignettes of cities, mountains, and landmarks
- Latitude and longitude grid where historically appropriate
- Rhumb line network for nautical charts

### Script Documentation (each script must be illustrated)

**Kufic:**
- Geometric, angular letterforms
- Horizontal extension emphasis
- No diacritical marks (early Kufic)
- Used in early Qur'an manuscripts, architecture, coinage
- Proportional measurement based on square module
- Variations: Simple Kufic, foliated Kufic, plaited Kufic, geometric Kufic, Eastern Kufic
- Color: Dark brown/black ink, gold illumination, warm parchment
- Construction: Ruler and set square, precise angles
- Do: Show proportional grid, letter block construction, architectural application in mosque
- Don't: Fluid, cursive forms; modern decorative distortions

**Thuluth:**
- Large, elegant cursive script
- One-third of each letter slopes (etymology of "one-third")
- Used for architectural inscriptions, Qur'an headings, decorative panels
- Deep curved bowls, sweeping descenders
- Proportional: Height of alif = 6-9 dots
- Diacritical marks decorative and prominent
- Ornamental potential greatest of all scripts
- Color: Black with gold, red accents, blue elements
- Do: Show proportional dot system, decorative composition, mosque inscription panel
- Don't: Cramped spacing; overly complicated arrangements

**Naskh:**
- Clear, readable, round script
- Used for Qur'an text body, books, letters
- Developed by Ibn Muqla and perfected by Ibn al-Bawwab
- Small cursive letters with even spacing
- Proportional: Dot-based system
- Minimal decoration for maximum readability
- Color: Black ink, red for verse markers, cream paper
- Do: Show full alphabet with stroke order, Qur'an page layout, proportional dot system
- Don't: Excessive decoration reducing readability

**Diwani:**
- Highly cursive, complex script
- Developed in Ottoman chancery
- Letters densely interconnected
- Diacritical marks decorative and integrated
- Extremely difficult to read without training
- Used for official documents, royal decrees
- Variations: Diwani Jali (decorated)
- Color: Black and gold, sometimes colored diacritics
- Do: Show connected letter groups, jali decorative version, Ottoman firman example
- Don't: Unconnected simplified versions; contemporary casual use

**Maghribi:**
- Developed in Islamic West (Maghreb, Andalusia)
- Wide, flowing letters with deep descenders
- Round curves, exaggerated letter tails
- Unique letter forms (fa with dot below, qaf with one dot above)
- Used in Qur'ans of North and West Africa
- Warm, earthy aesthetic
- Color: Brown ink, gold and blue decoration, warm parchment
- Do: Show distinctive letter forms, Andalusian/North African Qur'an page, Maghrebi manuscript
- Don't: Confuse with other scripts; use non-Maghrebi letter forms

**Ruqah:**
- Simple, cursive everyday script
- Developed in Ottoman period
- Short verticals, flat tops to letters
- Used for personal correspondence, notes
- Most commonly used script in daily writing
- Least decorative, most functional
- Color: Black ink, plain paper
- Do: Show quick writing samples, letter heights, practical application
- Don't: Over-decorate; present as formal or monumental

**Ibn Muqla Proportional System:**
- Circle-based measurement: all letters derived from circle (circle diameter = alif height)
- Dot-based measurement: rhombic dot from qalam nib
- Alif = 6-7 dots height (depending on script)
- Systematic proportion across all letters
- Geometric construction underlying all scripts
- Color: Blue/orange construction lines, black letters, red measurements
- Do: Show circle construction, dot grid, letter overlays with construction
- Don't: Present as rigid rules (artistic flexibility allowed)

### Educational Goal
Enable learners to understand the principles of Islamic decorative patterns — their geometric foundations, natural inspiration, and symbolic meanings. ### Geometric Pattern Constructions

**4-point star:** Square grid, diagonal lines, star from overlapping squares. Used in tile work, screens, borders.

**6-point star:** Hexagonal grid, equilateral triangles, star from intersecting triangles. Common in woodwork, metalwork.

**8-point star:** Square grid rotated 45 degrees, khatam (seal) pattern. Most common Islamic geometric motif.

**12-point star:** Combination of squares and hexagons, complex radial symmetry. Found in major architectural monuments.

**Tessellation:** Regular division of plane using polygons. Girih tiles (decagram, pentagon, hexagon, etc.) used in complex patterns. Understanding of unit cell, repeat method, symmetry groups.

**Strapwork:** Geometric pattern with interwoven bands. Over-and-under indication. Border treatments.

**Architectural application:** Pattern scaled to building proportions, material appropriate (tile, stucco, wood, brick), relief depth consideration, color according to regional tradition.

**Color rules:** Traditional color families per region, contrast theory, ground vs. figure color relationships, metallic accent placement, border color sequences.

### Arabesque (Islimi) Motif Documentation

**Rumi leaf:** Stylized leaf form, symmetrical or asymmetrical, serrated or smooth edge, single or split, direction variants.

**Hatayi flower:** Composite flower forms — rose, tulip, hyacinth, carnation, lotus, peony. Symmetrical construction. Bud and full-bloom variants.

**Sarmash (vine scroll):** Continuous scrolling vine, leaf attachment points, spiral direction, branch points.

**Motif combination rules:** Scale relationships between motifs, spacing principles, symmetry types (bilateral, rotational, reflection), infinite repeat vs. finite composition.

**Background filler elements:** Small leaves, buds, dots — filling space without competing with primary motif.

**Shading and modeling:** Consistent light direction, color gradation, gold highlighting.

### Illumination (Tezhip) Documentation

**Gold leaf effect:** Warm yellow-gold base, burnished highlight, shadow with darker gold. Digital technique: gold gradient with noise for leaf texture.

**Lapis lazuli effect:** Deep ultramarine with pyrite flecks (small gold dots). Layered for depth.

**Border frames:** Anatomical parts — inner border, main border, outer border, corner pieces (koltuk). Proportional relationships.

**Chapter headings (surah basmalah):** Decorative cartouche, gold background, script integrated, arabesque surround, marginal ornament (hashiya).

**Background shading (tarama):** Diagonal parallel shading in gold or color, creating depth and texture.

**Color application order:** Gold first (burnish), then lapis (multiple layers), then other colors, then details.

- **Oil lamp:** Lamp body (#B8860B), Flame (#F39C12), Glow (#FEF9E7)
- **Abacus:** Frame (#8B4513), Beads (#E74C3C, #3498DB), Background (#F8F9FA)

### Lighting Model
Flat vector lighting with consistent upper-left highlight. Glow effects for lamps, stars, and calligraphy gold. Simple shadows (drop shadow or flat bottom shadow). No complex shading.

### Mood
- All loading illustrations: Calm, positive, beautiful, engaging
- Waiting transformed from frustration to pleasure
- Each illustration fits the feature's emotional character
- Gentle, never urgent or anxious
- Pride in platform craft

### Camera Angle
Flat front-on for all loading illustrations. Consistent centered framing. No perspective changes during animation.

### Visual Hierarchy
1. Animated subject: central, primary focus
2. Secondary action: supporting elements
3. Background: subtle, non-distracting
4. Color: brand-aligned, accessible

### Animation Specifications
- **Format:** Lottie (preferred), GIF (fallback), CSS (simple shapes)
- **Duration:** 2-3 seconds per loop
- **Frame rate:** 30fps for smooth animation, 60fps for complex
- **Max file size:** 100 KB (Lottie JSON), 500 KB (GIF)
- **Easing:** Ease-in-out for natural motion, linear for continuous (compass rotation)
- **Looping:** Seamless loop (start = end state)
- **Color count:** 2-4 colors maximum
- **Dimensions:** 200x200 px (standard), 400x400 px (hero), 120x120 px (compact)

### Accessibility Considerations
- Loading animation not essential for task completion
- prefers-reduced-motion: show static frame instead
- Alt text: "Loading [feature name]..." descriptive of animation content
- Animation frame rate safe for photosensitive users
- No flashing or rapid motion (max 2 motion events per second)
- Color contrast sufficient for static frame
- Screen reader: "Loading" announced, "Complete" when done
- Option to disable all loading animations in settings

### Educational Goal
Make waiting time valuable and delightful. Reinforce platform themes (knowledge growth, beauty, learning process). Communicate attention to detail and care for user experience.

### Do Examples
- Knowledge tree growing: Center frame, seed at bottom, sprout emerges over 1s, stem rises, leaves unfurl, sapling sways gently. Frame loop: grow, sway, reset. Simple shapes, gentle motion. Brown and green palette. Warm background.
- Calligraphy being written: Right-to-left motion, reed pen angled, ink flowing from nib, elegant letter taking shape, diacritical marks added as dots, word completes, pen lifts, brief pause, next word begins. Cream parchment background. Gold accent on decorative marks.
- Book pages turning: Open book slightly angled, right page lifts at corner, turns over to left, settles flat, brief pause, next page begins same motion. Smooth physics, slight shadow during turn. Cream and brown palette.

### Don't Examples
- Spinners, progress bars, or loading dots (too generic)
- Animation that takes longer than the actual load
- Complex animation that affects performance
- Colors that clash or are hard to see
- Looping that's jarring at loop point
- Motion that could trigger vestibular disorders
- Negative imagery (broken, empty, sad)
- Animation that doesn't relate to the feature loading

### Production Specifications
- **Canvas size:** 200x200 px (standard); 400x400 px (hero); vector-based in After Effects
- **Format:** Lottie JSON (primary), GIF (fallback), WebP (animated)
- **Frame count:** 60-90 frames per loop (2-3s at 30fps)
- **Max file size:** Lottie 100KB, GIF 500KB
- **Software:** After Effects with Bodymovin (Lottie), Illustrator (vector source)
- **Export settings:** Trimmed to animation bounds, no backgrounds, optimized paths
- **Naming convention:** LOAD_ModeName_Version_Date

### Review Criteria
- Animation smooth and visually delightful
- Relevant to the loading feature
- Performance within file size limits
- Loop seamless (no visible start/end)
- Color palette limited and harmonious
- No motion safety issues
- Accessibility: reduced-motion alternative available
- Consistent with platform illustration style
- Animation specs documented
- Tested on low-end devices for performance
- Reviewed by motion designer

## 28. Error Illustrations

### Purpose
Transform error moments from frustrating to warm and human. Error illustrations acknowledge something went wrong while reassuring the user and guiding them toward a solution. Never blame the user. Always offer a path forward with a gentle, often whimsical illustration.

### Style
Warm, friendly, slightly whimsical illustration. Simple shapes, soft colors, gentle expressions. Characters and objects in slight distress but never broken or angry. Subtle visual metaphor for the error type. Limited palette (3-5 colors) for quick loading even on error pages. Style consistent with platform illustration system.

### Perspective
Centered, flat illustration. Subject (character, object) in center facing viewer. Simple background (solid or soft gradient). Clear silhouette. Room for text (error message, action button) below or beside.

### Composition Rules
- Subject centered, 60-70% of frame height
- Simple background with subtle texture or gradient
- Subject expresses error state visually (tilted, dimmed, wilted)
- Never show subject completely broken or destroyed
- Space for error title (prominent) and description (below)
- CTA button space at bottom of frame
- Supportive, warm color palette
- Minimal detail — loads fast even offline

### Error Illustrations

**General Error — "Something went wrong":**
- Book slightly tilted on shelf
- Book leaning at 15 degrees, bookmark hanging loose
- Warm amber background, brown book, red bookmark
- Not alarming — just a little out of place
- 4 colors: warm background, book, bookmark, shadow

**Network Error — "Can't connect":**
- Bird perched on a wire
- Wire gently sagging, bird looking at viewer
- Blue sky background, one small cloud
- Bird slightly ruffled but calm
- 4 colors: sky blue, bird, wire, cloud

**Offline — "You're offline":**
- Floating island with a single tree
- Island floating in space, tree with small nest
- Gentle stars around
- Peaceful, not isolating
- 4-5 colors: island earth, tree green, sky dark, stars, nest

**Not Found (404) — "Page not found":**
- Paper boat floating on gentle waves
- Boat slightly crumpled but still sailing
- Warm blue water, cream boat
- Adventurous, not lost
- 4 colors: water, boat, wave foam, background

**Server Error (500) — "Our fault":**
- Lamp with flickering flame
- Lamp base solid, flame small but steady
- Warm orange/amber glow
- "We're working on it" feeling
- 4 colors: lamp metal, flame, glow, background

**Permission Error — "Can't access":**
- Closed book with lock
- Book beautiful but closed
- Small key nearby (hope)
- 4 colors: book cover, lock, key, background

**Upload Error — "Couldn't save":**
- Pen with ink drop fallen
- Pen on side, small ink pool
- Not dramatic — just a spill
- 4 colors: pen, ink, paper, background

**Empty/No Results (shared with Empty States):**
- Friendly character searching with magnifying glass
- Curious, not disappointed
- Small question marks floating
- 4 colors: character, magnifying glass, question marks, background

**Timeout — "Taking too long":**
- Hourglass with sand slow
- Sand trickling one grain at a time
- Warm patient colors
- 4 colors: hourglass wood, sand, glass, background

**Rate Limit — "Slow down":**
- Camel resting with a tea cup
- Camel sitting, small tea steam rising
- "Take a break" message
- 4 colors: camel, tea, steam, desert background

### Color Palette
- **General error:** Amber (#F39C12), warm brown (#8B4513), red (#E74C3C)
- **Network:** Sky blue (#87CEEB), bird grey (#95A5A6), white cloud
- **Offline:** Deep blue (#2C3E50), island green (#27AE60), star white
- **Not Found:** Blue water (#3498DB), paper cream (#F5DEB3), foam white
- **Server error:** Lamp bronze (#CD7F32), flame (#F39C12), warm glow (#FEF9E7)
- **Permission:** Book dark red (#C0392B), gold lock (#F1C40F), key silver (#BDC3C7)
- **Upload:** Pen dark (#2C3E50), ink blue (#3498DB), paper cream (#FDF2E9)
- **Empty results:** Character warm (#E67E22), magnifying glass (#BDC3C7), question marks (#95A5A6)
- **Timeout:** Hourglass wood (#8B4513), sand (#F1C40F), glass (#ECF0F1)
- **Rate limit:** Camel (#D2B48C), tea (#C0392B), steam (#ECF0F1), desert (#FDEBD0)

### Lighting Model
Soft, warm, diffused light. Upper-left key light. Gentle shadows — nothing harsh. Glow effects for candles, lamps, and hope elements. Consistent with warm, friendly tone.

### Mood
- **All errors:** Warm, understanding, reassuring, temporary
- **User action error:** Gentle guidance, no blame
- **System error:** Apologetic but confident in resolution
- **Offline:** Peaceful solitude, not isolation
- **Not found:** Adventurous, curious, opportunity to explore

### Camera Angle
Flat front-on for all error illustrations. Centered. Eye contact with character/bird/object when applicable.

### Visual Hierarchy
1. Illustration: primary emotional communication
2. Error title: clear, human-readable (not "Error 404")
3. Error description: helpful, solution-oriented
4. Action button: primary path forward
5. Secondary actions: additional options

### Animation Possibilities
- Book tilt: gentle wobble (general error)
- Bird: head tilt, feather ruffle (network)
- Flame: gentle flicker (server error)
- Ink drop: slow drip (upload error)
- Hourglass: single sand grain falling (timeout)
- Camel: tea steam rising (rate limit)
- Boat: gentle rock on waves (not found)
- Cloud: slow drift (offline)
- Question marks: gentle bob (no results)

### Accessibility Considerations
- Error state communicated by composition + color + text (not just illustration)
- Alt text: descriptive of the illustration metaphor and error context
- Motion sensitivity: reduced-motion variant (static illustration)
- Error message text: clear, readable font, high contrast
- CTA button: large touch target (44px+), clear label
- Screen reader: error announced, then illustration described
- Color contrast: text meets WCAG AA on background
- No error sound that startles

### Educational Goal
Transform error moments into brand-building opportunities. Show care and personality even when things go wrong. Maintain user trust through transparent, warm communication.

### Do Examples
- 404 page: Paper boat on waves centered, gentle animation, title "Page drifted away" in warm tone, description "This page has sailed off. Try navigating home.", blue button "Set sail home", floating island illustration in warm blues and creams
- Offline page: Floating island with tree, bird on branch, stars around, title "You've floated off the map", description "No internet connection. Don't worry — your progress is saved.", offline-capable features listed, reconnect button, warm deep blue and green palette
- General error page: Tilted book on shelf, warm amber tones, title "Something tipped over", description "A small hiccup. Try again in a moment.", retry button, help link, comforting and non-technical language

### Don't Examples
- Error illustrations that blame the user (broken computer, tangled wires)
- Red alerts, exclamation marks, danger symbols
- Technical error codes as primary message
- Sad, angry, or frustrated characters
- Dead/broken imagery (dead plant, shattered objects)
- Motion that increases anxiety (flashing, shaking)
- Dismissive or robotic tone
- No clear path forward (missing CTA)

### Production Specifications
- **Canvas size:** 400x400 px (standard); 600x400 px (with text spacing); 200x200 px (inline)
- **DPI:** 144 DPI retina; 72 DPI standard
- **File format:** SVG (primary, scalable), PNG (fallback), Lottie (animated variants), WebP (animated)
- **Layers:** Background, Main Subject, Details, Effects, Text spacing guide
- **Animation specs:** Gentle motion only — wobble, float, flicker, drip. Nothing faster than 1s cycle.
- **Naming convention:** ERR_TypeName_Version_Date

### Review Criteria
- Error communicated with warmth, not frustration
- Visual metaphor appropriate to error type
- Color palette warm and accessible
- Animation gentle and optional
- Alt text descriptive and helpful
- CTA clear and prominent
- Brand personality consistent
- Motion safety checked
- Offline variant loads without network
- Tested on error pages across platform
- Reviewed by UX writer and designer

## 29. Empty States

### Purpose
Design beautiful, welcoming empty states that guide users toward action. Empty states occur on first visit, no-results searches, empty collections, and zero-progress views. Each empty state is an opportunity to welcome, explain, and motivate — never a dead end.

### Style
Warm, inviting, encouraging illustration. Clean composition with prominent CTA. Illustration centered above copy. Limited palette (3-5 colors) matching feature context. Character or object in center, looking at or pointing toward action. Style consistent with platform illustration system but slightly simpler for empty state context.

### Perspective
Flat, centered, front-facing illustration. Character faces viewer or looks toward CTA. Object centered with directional cue. Simple background (solid, soft gradient, subtle pattern). Clear space for text below.

### Composition Rules
- Illustration centered in upper 60% of card
- Title below illustration (14-18px, bold)
- Description below title (12-14px, regular)
- CTA button below description (primary action)
- Secondary link optional (learn more, explore)
- Maximum 3 lines of text
- Generous white space
- Illustration fills available width (max 300px)
- Color palette warm and welcoming

### Empty State Illustrations

**First Visit — "Welcome to the platform":**
- Open book with light emanating
- Pages glowing, small stars rising
- Character silhouette approaching the book
- Warm gold and blue palette
- Title: "Your journey begins here"
- Description: "Discover the rich heritage of Islamic knowledge"
- CTA: "Start exploring"

**No Results — "No matches found":**
- Character with magnifying glass
- Small question marks floating
- Curious expression, not disappointed
- Soft blue and warm grey palette
- Title: "No results found"
- Description: "Try adjusting your search or browse categories"
- CTA: "Browse all topics" + "Search tips"

**No Bookmarks — "Nothing saved yet":**

**No Progress — "Start your first lesson":**

**No Courses — "Learning plan empty":**

- CTA: "Learn something new"

### Color Palettes
- **Welcome:** Gold (#F39C12), Blue (#2980B9), White, Warm cream
- **No results:** Blue (#3498DB), Grey (#95A5A6), White, Warm accent
- **No bookmarks:** Beige (#F5DEB3), Red (#E74C3C), Warm brown
- **No progress:** Green (#27AE60), Blue (#3498DB), Sun yellow (#F1C40F)
- **No courses:** Wood (#8B4513), Cream (#FDF2E9), Warm brown
- **No achievements:** Gold (#F1C40F), Navy (#2C3E50), White
- **No friends:** Coral (#E17055), Teal (#1ABC9C), White
- **No notifications:** Purple (#9B59B6), Gold (#F1C40F), Light lavender
- **No garden:** Green (#27AE60), Brown (#8B4513), Water blue (#74B9FF)
- **No reviews:** Achievement green (#27AE60), White, Gold checkmark

### Lighting Model
Soft, warm, diffused upper-left light. Gentle glow on focal elements (book light, sparkle, sun). No harsh shadows. Warm ambient feel.

### Mood
- **All empty states:** Welcoming, encouraging, opportunity-focused
- **First visit:** Exciting, beginning of journey
- **No results:** Helpful, not disappointing
- **No bookmarks:** Practical, helpful guidance
- **No progress:** Motivating, low barrier to start
- **No achievements:** Aspirational, clear path
- **No connections:** Social invitation, warm
- **All reviewed:** Accomplished, satisfied

### Camera Angle
Flat front-on. Characters face viewer or look toward CTA. Objects centered and fully visible. No extreme angles.

### Visual Hierarchy
1. Illustration: emotional hook, draws attention
2. Title: communicates state clearly
3. Description: explains and guides
4. CTA button: primary action desired
5. Secondary link: alternative exploration

### Animation Possibilities
- Welcome book: gentle page glow pulse
- No results question marks: gentle bob
- Bookmark: floating settle into place
- Sprout: gentle growth wobble
- Shield star: occasional sparkle
- Connection spark: gentle pulse between characters

### Accessibility Considerations
- Alt text: describes illustration, conveys tone, suggests action (e.g., "Illustration of an open book glowing with light, welcoming the learner to begin their journey")
- CTA button: large touch target, clear label
- Color contrast: text meets WCAG AA
- Screen reader: state announced, description read, CTA focused
- Animation respects prefers-reduced-motion
- Text size readable at all viewport sizes
- No reliance on illustration alone to convey message

### Educational Goal
Transform empty states from dead ends into welcoming invitations. Guide users toward meaningful first actions. Reduce friction and anxiety when starting new features. Make every state feel intentional and cared for.

### Do Examples
- First visit welcome: Full card with open book illustration, gold light emanating upward, small character approaching, warm cream background, title "Your journey begins here", description "Discover the rich heritage of Islamic knowledge through guided lessons and explore at your own pace.", primary CTA "Start exploring", secondary link "Learn more about the platform"
- No search results: Character with magnifying glass, gentle curious expression, question marks floating, soft blue background, title "No results found", description "Try different keywords or browse our topic categories", CTA "Browse all topics", secondary "Search tips"
- No garden plants: Garden bed with soil, seed packet, water droplet, green and brown warm palette, title "Your garden is ready", description "Complete lessons to plant knowledge seeds. Review regularly to help them grow.", CTA "Start learning"

### Don't Examples
- Empty states that look broken or incomplete
- Sad, lonely, or disappointed characters
- Cold, corporate empty states ("No data available")
- Missing CTA or unclear next step
- Generic empty states across features (each should be specific)
- Dismissive tone ("Nothing here yet") instead of inviting
- Technical language in descriptions
- No visual connection to the feature's theme
- CTA that leads to dead end or requires prior action
- Illustration that loads slowly (defeats the purpose)

### Production Specifications
- **Canvas size:** 400x300 px (standard card); 600x400 px (hero); 200x150 px (compact)
- **DPI:** 144 DPI retina; 72 DPI standard
- **File format:** SVG (primary, scalable), PNG (fallback), Lottie (animated variants)
- **Layers:** Background, Main Subject, Details, Effects, Text Spacing Guide
- **Text spacing guide:** Guide layer showing title, description, CTA bounding boxes
- **Animation specs:** Gentle, 2-3s loop, under 50KB Lottie
- **Naming convention:** EMPTY_TypeName_Version_Date

### Review Criteria
- Welcoming tone communicated through illustration and copy
- Clear call to action present and prominent
- Feature-appropriate visual metaphor
- Color palette warm and inviting
- Copy encouraging and specific
- Animation gentle and optional
- Alt text descriptive and helpful
- Accessibility requirements met
- Loads quickly (critical for empty states)
- Consistent with platform illustration style
- Tested across all empty state scenarios
- Reviewed by UX writer and designer
